# GDV Evaluation Summary

A small formative evaluation was conducted with three football-interested participants. The goal was to check whether the final static visualizations communicate the intended insights clearly.

The participants answered five short tasks about build-up types, attack duration, team comparison, finishing efficiency and the Spain case study.

Overall, the build-up type chart and the direct-vs-patient team comparison were easiest to understand. Participants could identify that quick attacks were the most common build-up type and could name teams with more direct or more patient goal build-ups.

The efficiency vs tournament progress chart required the most explanation. The main risk was that viewers could interpret the chart as causal. Therefore, the report text was revised to state clearly that the figure shows an association, not causation.

The Spain case study was understandable, but the numbered pass sequence needed a short explanation. The final report now explains that the numbers show the order of successful passes before the goal.

The stacked build-up type chart also needed clearer category definitions. Therefore, the report explains Quick, Medium and Long before interpreting the chart.
