# GDV Evaluation Tasks - Final Version

## Goal

This short formative evaluation checks whether the final static GDV visualizations communicate the intended insights for amateur football training clearly.

## Participants

Three football-interested participants are sufficient for this small formative evaluation. They do not need professional analytics knowledge, but they should understand basic football terms such as pass, shot, goal and final third.

## Procedure

Show the final figures in the same order as in the report:

1. Passes before shots and goals
2. Goal conversion rate by pass category
3. Teams with shots after final-third entries
4. Entry method into the final third
5. Start zones of goal attacks
6. Team Directness Ranking
7. Spain final-third entries and chance quality

Ask each participant to think aloud while answering the tasks. Write down misunderstandings, unclear labels, and comments about the design.

## Tasks

### Task 1 - Pass categories

Look at Figure 1. Do shots and goals at the FIFA World Cup 2022 seem to happen more often after few passes or after long passing sequences?

### Task 2 - Efficiency

Look at Figure 2. Which pass category has the highest goal conversion rate? Does this mean this category is always the best attacking strategy?

### Task 3 - Final-third entries by team

Look at Figure 3. Name one team that often turns final-third entries into shots. What does the difference between shot rate and goal rate tell you?

### Task 4 - Entry method

Look at Figure 4. Which entry method leads most often to shots after entering the final third? Is this enough evidence to say that this method is always better?

### Task 5 - Start zones

Look at Figure 5. From which simplified zones do goal attacks start in the different pass categories?

### Task 6 - Team directness

Look at Figure 6. Name one team that appears more direct and one team that appears more patient. Is this a quality ranking or a style comparison?

### Task 7 - Spain use case

Look at Figure 7. Can you understand what the lines, green zones and xG labels mean? What training idea could an amateur coach take from this plot?

## Feedback questions

1. Which figure was easiest to understand?
2. Which figure was most confusing?
3. Were the titles, labels and legends clear?
4. Did the visualizations answer the research question?
5. What would you improve before submission?
