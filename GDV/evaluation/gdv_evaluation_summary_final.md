# GDV Evaluation Summary - Final Version

A small formative evaluation was conducted with three football-interested participants. The goal was to check whether the final static GDV visualizations communicate the intended insights for amateur football training clearly.

The participants answered tasks about pass categories, goal conversion rate, final-third entries, entry methods, start zones, team directness and the Spain use case. The evaluation was not designed as a statistical study. Its purpose was to identify misunderstandings and improve the final visual design.

Overall, the first two figures were easiest to understand. Participants quickly understood that many shots and goals happen after few or medium-length passing sequences. The Team Directness Ranking was also understandable because the sorted lollipop design made the ranking clear.

The more complex figures required more explanation. In the entry-method figure, participants needed a reminder that a higher shot rate does not prove that long passes are always better. In the Spain use-case figure, participants understood the idea only after xG and the meaning of the green zones were explained directly.

The main design risk was causal interpretation. Viewers could read higher conversion rates or higher shot rates as proof that one attacking method is always superior. Therefore, the report was revised to state clearly that the analysis is descriptive and does not prove causality.

The following improvements were applied after the evaluation:

1. Category definitions were explained before interpreting the figures.
2. Titles and captions were made more explicit.
3. xG was explained directly in the Spain figure and in the report text.
4. The text now states that Penalty-Shootouts were excluded and that in-game set pieces remain part of the attacking context.
5. The limitations section was strengthened to mention event-data limits, missing off-ball movement and the limited transferability from professional football to amateur training.
